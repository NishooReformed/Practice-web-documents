Atoms are the fundamental unit of matter

Learn about atoms more on Wikipedia

An atom is the smallest constituent unit of ordinary matter that has the properties of a chemical element. Every solid, liquid, gas, and plasma is composed of neutral or ionized atoms. Atoms are extremely small; typical sizes are around 100 micrometers 100 picometers (a ten-billionth of a meter, in the short scale).

The atom consist of following three particles:

  • Proton
  • Electron
  • Neutron

Atom's particle's mass in order -

  1. Neutron
  2. Proton
  3. Electron
